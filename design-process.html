01
01 — Problem Definition
Understanding the biomechanical gap.

Before drawing a single line, the project began with a literature review: how does the knee actually move? What torque does walking require? Where do existing braces fail? The answer — the instantaneous center of rotation traces a J-shaped curve, and every fixed-hinge device ignores this — became the architectural constraint everything else was built around.

Key findings
  • ICR migration of 12–18 mm from 0° to 90° flexion makes fixed-pin devices geometrically incompatible
  • Therapeutic torque threshold for gait: ≥ 8 Nm; no existing sub-$1,000 device reaches this
  • Target BOM ceiling set at $200 — below a typical insurance co-pay

02
02 — Ideation
Sketching the semi-rigid chain concept.

Three architectural concepts were sketched and evaluated: a telescoping rail system, a flexible sleeve with embedded actuators, and the semi-rigid chain. Only the chain inherently tracks a non-fixed rotation center — the cable tension collapses the slack links into a rigid arc whose radius of curvature follows the posterior joint geometry.

Dozens of chain geometries were sketched before settling on the 12-link PETG configuration. The key insight: link width controls the minimum bend radius, not the cable routing.

Key decisions
  • Semi-rigid chain selected over rigid rail — self-aligning, field-repairable, low mass
  • Hip-mounted actuator keeps distal mass under 250 g
  • Dyneema tendon chosen for 1.2 kN MBL at 1 mm diameter

03
03 — CAD Design
From sketch to parametric model.

The chain geometry was modeled parametrically so link count, width, and tendon channel diameter could be swept across a design space. FEA confirmed that PETG at 2.4 mm wall thickness withstands the peak cable load (≈ 180 N) with a 3× safety factor — printable on a consumer FDM printer in under 40 minutes per set.

The aluminium thigh and shank rails were designed for bending stiffness, not just weight. The 6061-T6 C-channel profile was chosen after calculating that square tubing would exceed the mass budget without adding meaningful rigidity in the relevant loading direction.

CAD decisions
  • 12 links at 22 mm width — covers 0°–145° with ≤ 1.4 mm mean ICR offset
  • 6061-T6 aluminium C-channel rails — 31 % lighter than equivalent steel
  • PETG links: 2.4 mm wall, printable without supports

04
04 — Prototyping
Building the first physical prototype.

Three prototype generations were built. Gen 1 used a single continuous tendon threaded through 3D-printed pillar links — the slack behaviour was correct but the link-to-link friction under tension caused non-uniform force transmission. Gen 2 switched to individual clevis joints; Gen 3 refined the tendon channel geometry to reduce friction by 60 % (measured by load cell).

Each iteration was bench-tested on the mannequin rig before the next generation was printed, keeping cycle time under 48 hours per iteration.

Iteration outcomes
  • Gen 1 → Gen 2: replaced pillar threading with clevis joints — eliminated binding at high flexion angles
  • Gen 2 → Gen 3: radiused tendon channel, PTFE liner — friction reduced 60 %
  • Final link count fixed at 12 after ICR alignment testing

05
05 — Testing
Validation against the design requirements.

Formal validation was conducted on a hinged anthropomorphic mannequin under six experimental conditions (initial leg angle ∈ {75°, 95°}, servo speed ∈ {9, 13.5, 20 °/s}). Three repeatability runs each. The ICR tracking was measured by imaging the joint against calibrated reference markers.

The AI pipeline was validated separately against five licensed physiotherapists across 22 annotated sessions. Agreement was scored on training stage classification and weekly load recommendation — 82 % match across both categories.

Validation results
  • Mean ICR misalignment: 1.39 mm across 0°–145° ROM — within the 2 mm design target
  • Peak torque: 13.5 Nm — exceeds 8 Nm therapeutic threshold
  • Force CV: < 2 % across repeatability runs — consistent actuation confirmed
  • AI–PT agreement: 82 % on stage classification and load recommendation
